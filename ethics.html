Ethics Reflections

Nemiah Hayes

Privacy, to me, is a concern of particular importance. The more that I have researched and delved into the ideas and prospects behind data privacy and protection, the more I believe that large tech companies such as Facebook, Google, and Microsoft aren’t holding up to their ethical and moral responsibilities. As large tech companies who deal with vast heaps of data, they have an obligation to upkeep and protect the data and personal information that they are gathering from us. Without regulation, however, it’s clearly seen particularly with the rise of social media, that companies will take as much information as they can in the name of profit. When companies are able to run rampant in the way that Facebook and Google are, it’s not just a reflection of those companies' wrongdoings but also on our governments for not upholding our human right to privacy. While it may seem that in a democracy like ours, that’s also a reflection of the people themselves, I believe it’s seen commonly in the media that politicians themselves are, at many times, unable to fully comprehend the measures that they must take in order to protect their people.

It’s a tough situation to look at as when privacy isn’t being overstepped, and data isn’t being abused, there’s actually a lot of wonderful technology that has come to be very helpful in the modern era behind it all. Compared to the wild west era or the internet, doing anything online is much more practical and streamlined. Thanks to technologies like device fingerprinting and cookies our experiences are much more accommodated to our liking. These technologies however are very powerful, and with that power comes great responsibility. While they’re very powerful, they’re very easy to abuse, and that goes especially for large companies whose websites have millions upon millions of hits on their sites every day. This also only makes the importance of regulation ever so much more relevant. The morals and ethics of data collection aren’t an issue of if it should be done, it’s an issue of how it should be done.
